<p><p>Reid Vapor Pressure (RVP) analyzers are categorized into standard pressure and high-pressure markets based on the pressure levels they operate under. Standard pressure RVP analyzers typically operate at atmospheric pressure, assessing the volatility of liquid hydrocarbons under normal conditions. In contrast, high-pressure RVP analyzers are designed for conditions above atmospheric pressure, accommodating the behavior of hydrocarbons under elevated pressures, which is crucial for specific industrial applications. These distinctions affect their use, calibration, and data interpretation in various sectors.</p></p>
